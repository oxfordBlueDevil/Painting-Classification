About neuralArt.io

The objective of classifying fine art paintings is highly complex. When studying a painting, an art expert can figure out its style, its genre, the artist, and the period to which it belongs.

At first thought, it is almost laughable to think that a machine can be able to classify paintings. And yet, neuralArt.io seeks to exactly do that.

Since the field of computer vision has made signficant leaps in getting digital systems to recognize and categorize objects and scenes in images and videos, today it's possible to make machines learn and judge such complex visual concepts and stylistic elements. The ultimate goal of neuralArt.io is to develop a Machine Learning model that is able to perform large-scale fine art painting classification and be capable to make semantic-level judgements, such as predicting a painting's genre (portrait or landscape) and its artist.

At the moment, using deep neural networks, our artist classifier can detect paintings of Van Gogh, Joseph Mallord Turner, and Cezanne, and our genre classifier can detect whether or not a painting is a portrait. Please find a painting of your own and test out our neural network classifiers on this app.
